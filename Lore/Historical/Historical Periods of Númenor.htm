Historical Periods of Númenor

By Peter Crijins, ported to the Lexicon by Atharôn.

    Arcadian Period – (S.A. 32 - 883)

  • Foundation – (S.A. 32 - 442)

    • The reign of Elerosse Tar-Minyatur; during this period, Númenor is founded and the King takes a

      • royal Quenya name, setting a tradition of Quenya being the royal language of the isle.

  • Classical Antiquity – (S.A. 442 - 883)

    • Tar-Vardamir to Tar-Meneldur; during this period, the Númenóreans sail back to Middle-earth for the

      • first time; in SA 750, crown prince Anardil, establishes Vinyalonde, the first permanent haven in

      Middle-earth.

    Golden Age – (S.A. 883 - 2737)

  • Colonisation – (S.A. 883 - 1869)

    • Tar-Aldarion to Tar-Minastir; during this period, the Númenóreans establish more permanent havens

      • and trading posts in Middle-earth and aid the Elves in the War of the Elves and Sauron, SA 1693 - 1701;

      sometimes referred to as the Age of Seafaring, or the Eldarin Alliance Period; also called the

      Early Middle Period.

  • Dominion – (S.A. 1869 - 2737)

    • Tar-Ciryatan to Tar-Alcarin; during this period, the King’s Men and the Elendili factions are

      • established, and the Númenóreans begin the military occupation of other lands. It also includes

      Herucalmo’s Usurpation and the Reign of the Illegitimate Sovereign, or the Interregnum; also called

      the Late Middle Period.

    Imperial Era – (S.A. 2737 - 3319)

  • Supremacy – (S.A. 2737 - 3177)

    • Ar-Belzagar (Tar-Calmacil) to Ar-Gimilzôr (Tar-Telemnar); during this period, the Kings begin to use

      • Adûnaic names and to persecute the Faithful; sometimes called the Dark Days of Númenor by the Faithful

      historians and the Age of Splendour by King’s Men historians.

  • Revival – (S.A. 3177 - 3255)

    • The reign of Tar-Palantir; during this period, the King endeavours to bring back the old traditions

      with his Reforms, also assuming a royal Quenya name; sometimes referred to as the Days of

      Restoration by Faithful historians and the Reign of the Apostate by King’s Men historians.

  • Against the Powers – (S.A. 3255 - 3319)

    • The reign of Ar-Pharazôn (Tar-Calion); during this period, the King defeats the Maia Sauron, making

      • him his servant, who convinces the King to worship Morgoth and to attack Aman, resulting in the

      Downfall of Númenor; sometimes called the Rule of the Fallen Usurper or the Disaster of the West, or

      the Last Days of Elenna, by Faithful historians and the Time of the Golden Glory or the Invasion for

      Immortality, or the Last Days of Gimlad, by King’s Men historians.

    Days after the Downfall – (S.A. 3319 - 3441)

  • Exile – (S.A. 3319 - 3441)

    • Fall of Númenor to the death of Elendil and the End of the Second Age; historiography of the Realms

      • in Exile begins.
