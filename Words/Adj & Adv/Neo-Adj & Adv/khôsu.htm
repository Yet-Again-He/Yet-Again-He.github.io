khôsu adj.  (Strong-II Neuter) “harsh, brutal”

 

Example:

Phazga ‘n Ar-Pharazônun Azra-zâin-nud mî khôsu.

“The rule of Ar-Pharazôn on the Sea-regions was harsh.”

 

Inflections

 

Nominative

khôsu

Plural

khôsî

 

 

Word-Formation:  It had to use -kh to start the word (due to the harshness of the sound).  The concept is darker in tone, so -u strengthened to -ô worked quite nicely.  It needed a second consonant to sound good, so in comes -s, since it is harsher of tone then others like -l or -m.  the final -o is to make it full-form, which is preferable with adjectives due to their inherently incomplete nature.
