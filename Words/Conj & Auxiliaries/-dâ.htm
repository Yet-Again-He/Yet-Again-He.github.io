-da suf. “verbal suffix”

The opposite of the agental suffix -ân, this is used to turn nouns into verbs.

 

Examples

ugru (“shadow”) à ugrudâ (“to overshadow”).

azgar (“war”) à azgarâ (“to (wage) war”). [1]

 

 

[1].  With a few exceptions, derived verbs cannot have more than three consonants, so in the case of azgar,  the [d] in the suffix disappears, leaving just -â.
